Player Characters

The four (five) people who ended up in Dexter for very different reasons.

Active Characters
FBI

Norris Cornwell

Special Agent — Washington D.C.
Vietnam vet. Former deep cover operative. His entire undercover crew was wiped out by a cult. Gets visions, especially on flights. Sent to Dexter by Director Webster to run down a KGB lead before the CIA can take the case.
Trauma: Vietnam + cult massacre (undercover op)
Family: Wife Gennifer · Ex-wife Jesse · Kids Phillip & Willie (Philly)
Tell: Reaches for his 8-track when he's fraying
CIA

Franklin Reed

Case Officer — Langley
Vietnam vet. Survivor of the Hanoi Hilton POW camp. Witnessed something supernatural in that prison — a winged entity that fed on prisoners. The government called it a disease. He knows what he saw. Sent by Director Casey to beat the FBI to Dexter.
Trauma: Hanoi Hilton — the Cryptid of Vietnam
Anchor: A priest he still communicates with (TBD)
Tell: Hypervigilant in crowds. Sees threats before they exist.
Academic

Professor Palmer

Anthropologist — New Mexico State University
Specialist in myth, religion, and the recurrence of cryptid gods and alien figures across human cultures. A true believer who masks it behind academic respectability. Close friend of Darnell Mantell. Called to Dexter by Sheriff Hopper after Mantell's death.
Connection: Last person Mantell called before he died
The voicemail: Confused. Wrong names. Wrong day. Ends singing happy birthday.
Tell: Quotes Mantell without realizing it.
Civilian

Tammy

Off the Grid — New Mexico Desert
Lives in a repurposed bus. Connected to the local Native tribe. Carries burn scars from a previous supernatural encounter. During a routine agricultural survey near Dexter, Coyote speaks to her for the first time — warning her away from a frozen, wrong-cold house in the desert. She finds what killed Mantell before anyone else does.
Spiritual: First contact with Coyote this session
The house: Frosted windows. Ajar door. Wrong cold.
The soil: Draws blood. Turns acrid yellow. Then nothing.
