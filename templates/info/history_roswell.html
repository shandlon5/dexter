The Roswell Incident

History File

In the summer of 1947, something came down outside Roswell, New Mexico—and the story changed fast. First it was reported as a recovered “flying disc.” Then it became a “weather balloon.” The quick reversal, the heavy military presence, and the missing details left a trail that never went cold. People who’ve followed the case don’t see a simple mix-up—they see a cleanup.

What the file suggests

  • Initial reporting described an unusual recovery—then the official explanation pivoted almost immediately.
  • Witness accounts and later statements describe materials that don’t behave like ordinary aircraft wreckage.
  • Paper trails exist, but key sections are missing, sealed, or heavily redacted.
  • The pattern looks less like confusion and more like a controlled narrative.

If you’re holding this file, you’re not looking for a neat answer—you’re looking for the parts they didn’t want kept.
