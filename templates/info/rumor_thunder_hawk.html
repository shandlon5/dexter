Thunder Hawk

Rumor File — wingbeats like a storm front, shadows across the desert.

Some swear they’ve seen a bird big enough to blot out the moon. Not an eagle. Not a condor. Something older, riding the wind like it owns it.

Sightings line up with sudden weather shifts — a clear sky turning ugly in minutes, a single crack of thunder with no storm on the radar, and a deep shadow crossing the ground like a low-flying plane with no sound of an engine.
